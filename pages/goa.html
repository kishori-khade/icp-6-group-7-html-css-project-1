The History of Baga Beach:-

  • The beach contains rows of shacks and fishing boats, and at high tide the beach is narrow.
  • The beach is named after the Baga Creek, which empties into the Arabian Sea at the north end of the beach
  • Baga became a traveler’s cliché in the ’60s and early’70s. It was the hippies who discovered the pristine beach.
  • They spread the word around resulting in hordes of European tourists descending on the golden sands of Baga. Thumbing or hitchhiking and travelling by cheap transport are things of the past.

Why to visit?:-

  • It's sandy beach and sunny weather atrract people from across the globe. It is the perfect destination for a short holiday..
  • Stunning Photography: The Baga beach offers incredible photo opportunities. It has multiple options for entertainment .
  • It's a great opportunity to motorboat ride,scuba diving,jetkiing,water skiling,banana ride,dolphin trip,water scooter,etc.
  • Baga beach is the perfect place to enjoy thrilling day and party late into night by visiting hangouts located right to beach.

Best Time to Visit:-

  • The best time to visit Agra is in the winter season between October to March.
  • There are no specific timings for Baga Beach..
  • Beachcombers can enjoy the beautiful sunset and then indulge in the legendery Baga Beach nightlife.
  • There is also a popular Baga Beach night market to pick up souvenirs.

How to reach ?

  • Air : The nearest airport is at Dabolim (50 kms)
  • Rail : The nearest railway station is at Karmali (17 kms) and Panaji (15kms). Express and Super fast trains run to Panaji from major cities of India.
  • Road : The Baga beach is just half an hour drive away from Panaji and 15 minutes from Mapusa. There are frequent buses available to Baga from Mapusa and Panaji throughout the day. From Panaji, the bus usually takes 30 minutes to reach Baga. Bicycles and motorcycles can be hired at many places in Baga.
